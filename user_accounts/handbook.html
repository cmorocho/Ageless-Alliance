Volunteer Handbook

The wonderful Ageless Alliance team has provided you with a handbook filled with helpful articles. We hope you find these articles below useful in answering all of your questions and concerns about Ageless Link. Within each article is a comment section in which you and your fellow volunteers can share trustworthy advice and recommendations. If you are unable to find a solution in this handbook, please contact the Ageless Alliance team here.

Keep in mind that this handbook is meant to provide solutions to your questions, as well as establish community spirit among people who care about aging and dignity. We ask you to contribute to the comment sections with respect and helpfulness.

  • I've Been Paired with an Older Adult. What Now?

    1) We will share with you the older adult’s name, address, phone number, and any additionalinformation needed.

    2) A meeting time will be set up with you, the older adult, and a designated Ageless Alliance employee.

    3) The first meeting is all about introductions. The Ageless Alliance employee will also confirm that the older adult understands who we are, and would like to be a part of Ageless Service Corps.

    4) From there, you both can set up a time going forward to meet.

    5) The Ageless Alliance employee will contact the older adult twice in the first month, and once a quarter after that.

    6) Let your Ageless Alliance Supervisor know of any changes, questions, or concerns. We’re here to help and support!

  • Role of Ageless Volunteer
  • Who Is an Ageless Service Corps Volunteer

    Volunteers are lay or professional individuals who offer and provide their services to Ageless Alliance without compensation.

    The Ageless Service Corps Volunteer is a trained individual who provides professional support service to seniors in need. Volunteers embody the essence and heart of Ageless Alliance and are essential to the successful operation of the Ageless Service Corps Program.

    The first conversation is always hard. "Will they like me?" "Will I be able to help?" "What will I do?" These questions are natural. They will let you know what they need, and you, in turn will let them know what you can offer. The relationship unfolds step-by-step in a natural way.

  • Volunteer Guidelines

    Here are some guidelines that will help:

    1) Be Genuine - be yourself! Aside from modifications in behavior to assure the comfort of the participant, i.e. questions, less talking and more listening, conscious attentiveness, etc. the volunteer should relate the same "personality" he/she displays in any other situation.

    2) Communication - the volunteer supervision. The importance of clear communication between volunteer supervision and volunteers cannot be overemphasized. The two of you are a mini-team and can be of great help to one another by maintaining regular contact and keeping one another current on what is going on.

    3) Dependability - never offer more than you can deliver. To people in crisis, whose lives are subject to so much unpredictability, it is essential to know that they can count on someone or something.

    4) Be Accommodating - meet them where they are! Over and over again in your training, the concept of tuning in to the elder and meeting them in terms of their own values and life patterns is stressed.

    5) Little Things Mean a Lot!

    • * Your personal grooming and manor of dress can affect an elder's mood.
    • * Perfume or strong after-shave can be most unpleasant and many people have allergies or respiratory issues.
    • * A positive attitude and pleasant expression means a lot.
    • * Sometimes not talking, but sitting with a a person and letting him or her know you care by being there is the greatest gift of all.

  • Policies & Procedures
  • Responsibilities and Behavior

    ResponsibilitiesAdvocate volunteers play a pivotal role in enhancing the quality of care delivered to Ageless Alliance’s elders. The advocate’s role is to provide a source of support and companionship for elders who have nowhere else to turn. Therefore, an advocate’s responsibilities will vary depending on the needs of the individual elder. An advocate may be called upon to do anything from keeping the elder company in his or her home to transporting the elder to a doctor’s appointment. The advocate may also be asked to utilize their skills and training, as well as work with other volunteers to support the elder. Volunteers are expected to work closely with their case supervisor to ensure clarity of expectations and role.

    Behavior
    All Ageless Alliance volunteers should observe professional ethics in their interactions with other volunteers and elders. All volunteers should strive to extend common courtesy and maintain a professional demeanor regardless of rank or position. Although we are staffed by volunteers, Ageless Alliance strives to create an environment that is highly professional. For this reason, conduct in violation of the policies below, or otherwise detrimental to the security, personal safety, volunteer welfare or Ageless Alliance’s interests may lead to disciplinary action, up to an including termination of a volunteer’s relationship with Ageless Alliance.

  • Conflict Resolution - Do You Have a Complaint or Greivance?

    At some time during your Ageless Alliance service, you may have a complaint or grievance about your volunteer conditions or the treatment you are receiving. Volunteers and staff members should make every attempt to reach a solution at the appropriate level, involving a minimal number of fellow volunteers or staff members. You will not be subject to reprisal for filing a grievance. If, after exhausting other means to address your concerns you wish to file a formal grievance, please contact our Ageless Action Director for details and procedures. Note: grounds for grievances may include the violation of policies, unfair situations where no policies exist, and unfair or discriminatory policies. The grievance procedure is not the appropriate vehicle for resolving interpersonal conflicts.

  • Confidentiality Policy

    Confidential Information about Ageless Alliance, its volunteers, and elders is to be kept confidential and disclosed only to individuals within Ageless Alliance with a need to receive, and who are authorized to receive, such information, unless otherwise required by law. If in doubt as to whether information should be disclosed, err in favor of not disclosing information and discuss the situation with your case supervisor.

    All records and files maintained by Ageless Alliance are confidential and remain the property of Ageless Alliance at all times. Confidential information includes, but is not limited to, financial records, volunteer records (regarding current or past volunteers), email lists, elder lists, information regarding elder transactions, elder account information, elder medical or health information, other information regarding elders, any documents or information regarding Ageless Alliance operations, procedures or practices and any other proprietary or private information.

    Volunteers may be exposed to an elders’ private medical information, but should make every effort to leave the immediate area when an elder and medical staff are having a conversation related to the elder’s medical status. The volunteer should not ask the elder or any medical staff questions related to an elder’s diagnosis, insurance coverage, or anything else that relates to an elder’s health information.

    If you learn any information that leads you to believe that an Ageless Alliance Elder may be involved in some way, directly or indirectly, in the abuse or neglect of a child, elderly person or disabled person or if the elder is the victim of abuse or neglect, you must contact your case supervisor at Ageless Alliance immediately. Together, a report will be made to the appropriate agency.

    Confidential information obtained during or through volunteering with Ageless Alliance may only be used for the purpose for which the information is disclosed, and it may not be used by any volunteer for the purpose of furthering current or future outside employment or for obtaining personal gain or profit. Ageless Alliance reserves the right to avail itself of all legal or equitable remedies to prevent impermissible use of confidential information or to recover damages incurred as a result of such impermissible use of confidential information. The Volunteer’s confidentiality obligations with respect to confidential information obtained during or through volunteering with Ageless Alliance continue after he or she is no longer volunteering for Ageless Alliance.

    Each Volunteer shall be required to execute a Confidentiality Agreement and the above Confidentiality Policy shall be complied with in addition to, and not in the place of, the terms and conditions of the Confidentiality Agreement.

  • Safety
  • What is a Boundary?

    Boundaries are expectations or guidelines for a relationship that makes it possible to relate in healthy ways. Boundaries make it possible to be present and emotionally involved with others without becoming overwhelmed. They make relationships safer and more predictable for everyone.

    Boundaries are meant to help us and our participants

    • * They allow the participant to feel comfortable with us, knowing what to expect. They help us to stay on task.
    • * They prevent burnout.
    • * They make work less burdensome.
    • * They promote mutual respect.


    Exceeding Boundaries Signals I know I’m exceeding my boundaries when:
    • * My stress increases…I feel emotionally on edge with my own family and friends
    • * I find myself thinking about the participant too frequently
    • * I feel like I want to take over
    • * I feel like the participant is MY responsibility
    • * I lose objectivity…for instance, I become resentful toward a team member (even if I don’t openly express it)


    Common boundary violations
    • 1. Visiting more often or providing more services than the plan discussed with the Ageless Alliance Team and Director. Examples include giving money to the participant or family, or taking on roles that should be filled by others.
    • 2. Sharing excessively about our personal lives. Some sharing can be helpful in building trust and rapport with the participant. Too much sharing can become uncomfortable or can leave the participant feeling burdened. It is important to ask yourself, “How will this information help the participant?”
    • 3. Breaking confidentiality. Take care with participant information, including refraining from talking about participants in public areas like restaurants or other public places.
    • 4. Looking to the client to meet our emotional needs. It is true that we often “receive back” from participants emotionally and feel that our time with them has been personally fulfilling. However, this fulfillment will not always be present and it is not the client’s responsibility to provide. Our relationships with participants are not equal or reciprocal. Rather, the Service Corps Team is focused on the participants’ needs and works to provide them with care and support.


    The importance of boundaries Poor boundaries can lead to frustration. When expectations are not clear and consistent, participants can feel let down or misled. We may feel tired and stretched beyond our limits. Conversely, appropriate boundaries help us to serve our participants effectively, allowing everyone to feel clear and comfortable in the relationship. We are able to do what we can without feeling that we must do everything. Boundary issues are very common, and your supervisor is here to help! Please do not hesitate to talk through situations with the Director of Volunteers.
